ex3

Possibly shutting down soon

Start speech

ex3 is on the verge of shutting down. It's been a fun experiment, but I have been focusing much more on other projects, quite irrevocably, chief among them, Voxity. The once-insatiable zest I harboured for Discord bots has been supplanted by pursuits more aligned with my current architectural sensibilities. ex3 has been my longest running project yet, surviving over 4 years, but I think the spark is finally dying out. ex3 is not fun anymore. I'm also just sick of paying for hosting, which, isn't much, but it bugs me every period.

Should ex3's decommission proceed as anticipated, the casualties are also:

  • pigeon (since 2024)
  • OG hastebin, since 2021 (on-and-off)
  • The Musixmatch proxy for Voxity (I'll figure something out...)

This is not the extirpation of ex3 seriatim, this is a full decomission of my server, extinguishing everything on it.

I never got to space; I never went mainstream. ex3 has never had a million users, a billion command executions in a day, no viral conflagration that swept the platform. What ex3 did accomplish, however, was something arguably more meaningful: it was a fun toolkit for me and my friends to use, as a bespoke utility suite, and making cameos in several of my friends' media. As far as I'm aware, by that particular metric, that was a success in its own right --- ex3 was an unqualified triumph. But ex3 is no longer sustainable, fun, or worth hosting. Now, you could argue, "why not just leave it alone?", and that's a fair point, but ex3 is 80% inspired by other bots, and 20% insane concoctions that I made myself. Those 20% are eminently portable to the web, and the 80%... the progenitors did better.

As for pigeon, that project has been a massive success amongst my friends and some communities that used it. As of writing this, and since tracking began in early 2025, pigeon has processed 45,000+ messages, users have reset their chat 3.5K times, and about 100 different people have tried it. That is what I call successful. But pigeon is also becoming unsustainable, and I have kinda forgot how to even navigate its codebase after months of inactivity. I have tried to make updates, but most of them failed; for example, when I switched the model from Gemini to Grok, it was a massive failure, and I eventually went back, saying "Grok 4 Fast is.. ironically not fast, and stupid".

So, yeah. If ex3 does end up shutting down, it was a fun ride, and I hope you enjoyed it as much as I did. ex3 will probably not last past the end of March, or perhaps April, at the outermost. Like I said, if ex3 goes down, the whole ship will too; effectively marking the end of my server-sided development "era".

And as for the Voxity MM proxy, I will likely make it into a Chrome/Firefox extension. That will be beneficial anyway, as the proxied requests don't come from a single server.

On a side note, I am kinda feeling like X and Elon Musk with how duplicated and confusing my names are. I'm sure you're aware: he's got X, SpaceX, xAI, Model X, and a child called X. I have EX3 Corp, this, ex3.lol (my legacy personal site; which some confuse for this bot), and several things called ex(number). Don't misconstrue my words: I just think I have too many things with the same name.

Thanks for reading, and more importantly, thanks for using ex3... if you did <3

– exe
